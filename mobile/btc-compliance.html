BTC INVESTMENT TRANSACTION & PAYMENT COMPLIANCE ADDENDUM
This BTC Payment Compliance Addendum outlines the acceptable on-chain settlement workflows, the verification
and reconciliation procedures for crypto-based purchases, and the anti-money-laundering protocols that BUYBIT
requires before recognizing deposited funds as cleared for trading access. All BTC settlements must conform to
the following technical and compliance standards:

- Settlement Origin: Funds must originate from an on-chain address associated with a verified identity matching the
  Trader’s institutional or personal records on file.
- Confirmations Required: BTC transfers must achieve a minimum of 3 network confirmations before the funds are
  accepted into the funding ledger. High-value transfers (≥ 1 BTC) may require up to 6 confirmations.
- Network Fees: The originating party is responsible for any network fees to achieve timely inclusion in the
  blockchain. BUYBIT will not subsidize miner fees.
- Reconciliation and Memo Fields: Transfers must include any required memo or reference fields when applicable.
  Failure to include reconciliation references may delay acceptance of funds.
- Sanctions Screening: All incoming address origins will be screened against international sanctions and watch lists.

Upon receiving cleared funds, the system will mark the corresponding funding ledger entry and trigger the compliance
certificate issuance which authorizes subsequent provisioning events. BUYBIT reserves the right to withhold access
pending manual review if irregular activity or mismatches are detected between the submission and KYC records.

FAILURE TO COMPLY:  Non-compliant settlements may be returned, delayed, or subject to administrative holds. BUYBIT
reserves all rights to reject or pause settlements while investigations are ongoing.

CONTACT: For payment reconciliation or hold disputation, contact compliance@buybit.example with the network
transaction hash and the account reference number.
